**Claim:** Muscles adapt to the demands placed on them, so the training stimulus must
gradually increase over time. Progression can come from more load, more reps, more sets, or
better technique/range — not load alone.

**Evidence:** ACSM's progression position stand establishes systematically increasing
demand as the basis for continued gains; adding volume over time is one validated lever
(Schoenfeld 2017), alongside load and rep progression.

**Takeaway:** When last session's targets were hit cleanly, nudge the next session up —
add reps within the target range, then add load when the top of the range is reached (double
progression). This is precisely the rule the app's workout creator follows: "nudge
weights/reps up where the last comparable session hit its targets." The coach should ground
progression suggestions in the user's own last-session numbers, not generic increments.
