**Claim:** Metabolic stress (the "burn"/pump from accumulated metabolites in higher-rep,
shorter-rest sets) can contribute to hypertrophy, but it is secondary to mechanical tension
and not a target in its own right.

**Evidence:** Schoenfeld's three-mechanism model lists metabolic stress alongside tension
and damage; later evidence positions it as a modest, tension-dependent contributor rather
than an independent driver (Schoenfeld 2010).

**Takeaway:** Higher-rep, shorter-rest "pump" work is a reasonable tool — especially for
smaller muscles and machine/isolation movements where loading heavy is awkward — but it
complements, not replaces, heavier tension-producing work. The coach shouldn't prescribe
burn-focused work as a substitute for progressive overload on the main lifts.
