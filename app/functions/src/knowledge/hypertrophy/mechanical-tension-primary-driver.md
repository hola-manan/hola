**Claim:** High mechanical tension applied to a muscle through a challenging range of
motion is the dominant stimulus for hypertrophy. Metabolic stress and muscle damage are
secondary contributors, not requirements.

**Evidence:** The classic three-mechanism model (Schoenfeld 2010) identifies mechanical
tension, metabolic stress, and muscle damage — but subsequent work shows tension is
primary: hypertrophy tracks with hard, tension-producing sets, while damage is not the
mediator of growth (Damas 2016).

**Takeaway:** Program hard sets with meaningful load and full effort — that is what
generates the tension that drives growth. Chasing "the pump" or soreness for their own sake
is misguided; they are byproducts, not goals. This is why the coach should judge a session
by hard working sets and progressive load, not by how sore or fatigued it felt.
