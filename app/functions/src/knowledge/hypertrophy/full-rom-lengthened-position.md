**Claim:** Training through a full range of motion — and especially loading the muscle in
its lengthened (stretched) position — tends to produce equal or greater hypertrophy than
short, partial reps done in the shortened position.

**Evidence:** Partial reps performed at long muscle lengths produced greater gastrocnemius
growth than full ROM, and clearly more than shortened-position partials, in a controlled
trial (Ottinger 2023); this aligns with a broader body of ROM research favouring the
stretched position.

**Takeaway:** Cue a full, controlled range with an emphasis on the deep/stretched portion
(e.g. a deep stretch on RDLs, dips, calf raises, incline curls). When time or fatigue forces
partials, bias them toward the lengthened end. The coach can flag consistently short ROM as
a likely reason a muscle is lagging despite adequate set counts.
