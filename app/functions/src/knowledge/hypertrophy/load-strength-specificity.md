**Claim:** Maximal strength (1RM) is best developed with heavy loads — roughly ≥80% 1RM,
in the 1–6 rep range — because strength adaptations are specific to the load and skill of
lifting heavy.

**Evidence:** While hypertrophy is load-agnostic, the same meta-analysis found clearly
greater 1RM gains from high-load training (Schoenfeld 2017); ACSM's position stand
recommends heavy loading for advanced strength progression (ACSM 2009).

**Takeaway:** Match load to goal. If the user's goal is strength, program the main lifts
heavy (low reps, longer rest, higher effort). If the goal is size, load can be flexible.
This distinction should shape the coach's weight prescriptions — an e1RM-derived %1RM for
strength intent, a wider load window for hypertrophy intent — and is why the app tracks
estimated 1RM per exercise as the anchor for prescribing weights.
