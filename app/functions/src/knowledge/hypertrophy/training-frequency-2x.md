title: "Train each muscle about twice per week"
domain: hypertrophy

muscles: [all]

sources:
  - ref: "Schoenfeld BJ, Ogborn D, Krieger JW. Effects of resistance training frequency on measures of muscle hypertrophy: a systematic review and meta-analysis. Sports Med 2016;46(11):1689–1697"
    url: "https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27102172/"
---
**Claim:** For a fixed weekly volume, training a muscle ~2×/week produces more hypertrophy
than hitting it all in one session. Frequency mostly matters as a tool for *distributing*
volume across sessions.

**Evidence:** A meta-analysis found that, when weekly sets are equated, twice-weekly
frequency beats once-weekly for growth; whether 3× beats 2× is unresolved (Schoenfeld
2016).

**Takeaway:** Spread each muscle's weekly sets over at least two sessions rather than one
punishing session. This keeps per-session volume in a recoverable range and lets each set
be higher quality. When the coach builds a split or a workout, it should ensure major
muscles recur ~2×/week across the cycle, not that any single day maximizes one muscle.
