**Claim:** Sets should be taken close to failure to grow well, but training *to* momentary
failure on every set is unnecessary and adds fatigue. Roughly 0–3 reps in reserve (RIR)
captures most of the benefit.

**Evidence:** A meta-analysis found only a trivial hypertrophy advantage for training to
failure vs stopping short, and closer-to-failure training was associated with slightly
*smaller* strength gains and more fatigue (Refalo 2023).

**Takeaway:** Cue effort, not annihilation: most working sets should end around 1–3 RIR.
Reserve true failure for isolation work or the last set of an exercise. When readiness is
low, backing off to ~2–3 RIR is a sensible way to keep stimulus while cutting fatigue —
consistent with the app trimming intensity on low-readiness days.
