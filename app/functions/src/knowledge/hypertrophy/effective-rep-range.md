**Claim:** Muscle can be built across a broad loading spectrum — roughly 5–30 reps per set —
as long as sets are taken close to failure. There is no single "hypertrophy rep range."

**Evidence:** A meta-analysis found similar hypertrophy between low-load (>15 rep) and
high-load (<15 rep) training when sets approached failure; load mattered for maximal
strength but not for growth (Schoenfeld 2017).

**Takeaway:** Choose reps by practicality and joint comfort, not dogma. The 8–15 range is
convenient for most accessory work (enough load to be efficient, enough reps to be safe and
controllable), but 5–8 and 15–30 both grow muscle when effort is high. This gives the coach
freedom to prescribe lighter, higher-rep work when an injury note or low readiness makes
heavy loading unwise, without sacrificing hypertrophy.
