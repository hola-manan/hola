**Claim:** Muscle damage — and the soreness (DOMS) that often accompanies it — is a
side effect of training, not a driver of growth. You do not need to be sore to have trained
productively.

**Evidence:** Early-program muscle protein synthesis was directed at *repairing* damage
rather than building new tissue; hypertrophy tracked with protein synthesis only once damage
was attenuated (Damas 2016). Soreness reflects unaccustomed stimulus, not superior stimulus.

**Takeaway:** Don't chase soreness or judge a session by how wrecked it felt. Novel
exercises cause more DOMS without more growth. The coach should reassure users that reduced
soreness over weeks is normal adaptation, not lost progress, and should not add volume or
novelty just to "feel it more." Judge progress by load, reps, and volume trends instead.
