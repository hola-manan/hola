**Claim:** A resistance session elevates muscle protein synthesis (MPS) for roughly 24–48 h
afterward; growth is the net result of many such elevations exceeding breakdown over weeks,
supported by adequate protein intake.

**Evidence:** MPS rises after training and, once early-program damage subsides, correlates
with hypertrophy (Damas 2016); protein feeding across the recovery window modulates the MPS
response (Areta 2013).

**Takeaway:** Growth is cumulative and slow — the outcome of consistent hard sessions plus
sufficient protein, not any single workout. This frames realistic expectations: the coach
should point to multi-week trends (e1RM, volume) rather than session-to-session noise, and
reinforce that the elevated-MPS window is best "fed" by hitting daily protein, not by any
magic post-workout timing (see nutrition cards).
