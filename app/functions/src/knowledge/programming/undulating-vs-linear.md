**Claim:** Both linear periodization (gradually rising intensity / falling reps over weeks)
and daily/weekly undulating periodization (varying intensity and reps more frequently) work.
Neither is clearly superior for most trainees.

**Evidence:** Meta-analysis confirms periodized > non-periodized for strength, but does not
establish a decisive winner between undulating and linear models; effects are small and
overlap (Williams 2017).

**Takeaway:** Pick the model that fits the schedule and preference, then run it
consistently. Undulating variation (e.g. a heavier lower-rep day and a lighter higher-rep
day for the same muscle within a week) pairs naturally with a rotating cycle. The coach
should not push a specific periodization brand — adherence and progression matter more than
the model label.
