**Claim:** Performance on an exercise is best when it's done while fresh. Place the
highest-priority and most demanding (usually multi-joint/compound) movements early in the
session; whatever comes last is done under fatigue and progresses slowest.

**Evidence:** ACSM's position stand recommends sequencing large-muscle, multi-joint
exercises before smaller-muscle, single-joint ones for strength and power development (ACSM
2009).

**Takeaway:** Front-load the lifts that matter most for the user's goal — if a lagging
muscle is the priority, its key exercise can even go first, fresh, rather than defaulting to
"big compound first." This is the rule the app's workout creator already applies ("4–7
exercises in sensible order, compounds first"); the coach can justify the ordering and
adjust it to bring up a lagging muscle.
