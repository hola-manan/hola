**Claim:** A brief, specific warm-up — light general movement plus a few ascending "ramp-up"
sets of the first exercise — primes performance and reduces injury risk, without adding
meaningful fatigue. Warm-up sets are not counted as working volume.

**Evidence:** ACSM's guidance includes appropriate warm-up as part of safe, effective
resistance-training prescription (ACSM 2009); ramp-up sets rehearse the movement and prepare
tissues at the working load.

**Takeaway:** Before a heavy compound, do 2–4 progressively heavier sets up to the working
weight; isolation and lighter machine work usually need only 1 light set or none. This is
why the app logs warm-up sets separately and excludes them from volume and e1RM stats — they
prepare you for the working sets that actually count.
