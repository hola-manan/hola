**Claim:** Planned variation of training variables over time (periodization) produces
somewhat larger maximal-strength gains than doing the same thing every session, though the
effect is modest.

**Evidence:** A meta-analysis of 18 studies found periodized programs outperformed
non-periodized ones for 1RM strength, with a small-to-moderate effect that shrank but
remained significant after adjustment (Williams 2017).

**Takeaway:** Structure beats randomness, but don't over-engineer it. A simple, coherent
plan that progresses and periodically varies intensity/volume is enough for most trainees;
elaborate periodization is not required for good results. The app's cycle + progressive
targets already provide lightweight structure — the coach should reinforce consistency and
progression over frequent program-hopping.
