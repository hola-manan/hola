**Claim:** Maintaining strength and muscle takes far less work than building it, and short
layoffs cause little loss. Reduced-frequency, reduced-volume training preserves gains for
weeks; a missed session or two is not a setback.

**Evidence:** Strength and muscle can be maintained with as little as ~1 session/week or a
third of the usual volume, provided intensity stays reasonably high, over multi-week periods
(Spiering 2021).

**Takeaway:** Travel, illness, or a busy stretch won't erase progress — reassure users and
prescribe a minimal maintenance dose (1×/week per muscle, a few heavy sets) rather than
worrying about "losing everything." Noticeable detraining takes weeks of near-total
inactivity. The coach should treat a short adherence gap as recoverable, not cause for
panic or a program overhaul.
