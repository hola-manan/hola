**Claim:** Resting ~2–3 minutes between hard sets of compound lifts produces greater
strength and hypertrophy than short (~1 min) rests, because it preserves the load and reps
you can do on the next set.

**Evidence:** In trained men, 3-minute rests beat 1-minute rests for both strength and
muscle thickness over 8 weeks (Schoenfeld 2016); ACSM recommends longer rests for
heavy/compound work (ACSM 2009).

**Takeaway:** Don't rush compounds — 2–3 min (longer for very heavy sets). Short rests
(60–90 s) are fine for isolation/machine work where recovering full performance matters less
and time efficiency helps. The app already stores a per-exercise rest target and the creator
sets `restSeconds`; the coach should default compounds to the longer end.
