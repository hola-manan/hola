**Claim:** Meaningful strength and muscle can be built (and easily maintained) with
surprisingly little volume when effort is high. The first few hard sets per muscle per week
deliver most of the benefit; more adds diminishing returns.

**Evidence:** The dose-response curve is steepest at low volumes (Schoenfeld 2017), and
performance can be *maintained* on a fraction of normal training (Spiering 2021).

**Takeaway:** When time is short, don't skip — do a reduced session. A couple of hard sets
per muscle beats zero, and keeps momentum. This reframes busy weeks: the coach should offer a
trimmed but effective workout rather than an all-or-nothing plan, protecting adherence, which
is the true driver of long-term results.
