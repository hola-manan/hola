**Claim:** No split (full-body, upper/lower, push-pull-legs, bro-split) is inherently
superior. A split is just a way to distribute weekly volume so each muscle is trained ~2×
and each session is a recoverable size.

**Evidence:** With weekly volume equated, growth is similar across splits; the frequency
meta-analysis shows the important variable is that each muscle is hit ~2×/week, which most
sensible splits accomplish (Schoenfeld 2016).

**Takeaway:** Choose the split by training days available and recovery, not by belief in a
"best" split. 3 days → full-body or upper/lower/full; 4 → upper/lower; 5–6 → PPL or
upper/lower variants. The right split is the one that lets the user hit each muscle's weekly
range ~2× and actually adhere. The app's cycle is the container for whichever split fits.
