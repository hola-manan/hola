**Claim:** Adaptations are specific to the demand imposed (Specific Adaptation to Imposed
Demand). Training a movement, muscle, load range, or rep range improves that quality most.

**Evidence:** Strength gains are largest at the trained load and in the trained movement,
while hypertrophy responds across loads (Schoenfeld 2017); ACSM's framework builds
prescription around matching variables to the goal (ACSM 2009).

**Takeaway:** Programming choices must follow the goal. Want a bigger bench? The bench (and
close variations) must be trained. Want balanced physique development? Every muscle needs
direct volume, because indirect work under-stimulates lagging muscles. This underpins the
app's per-muscle volume tracking: a muscle only progresses if it receives specific,
sufficient stimulus, and the coach should reason in those terms.
