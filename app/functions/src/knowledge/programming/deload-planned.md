**Claim:** Periodically reducing training stress (a "deload" — typically cutting volume
and/or intensity for ~1 week) lets accumulated fatigue dissipate so performance rebounds,
without meaningfully losing muscle.

**Evidence:** ACSM's framework incorporates planned variation and recovery to sustain
progression (ACSM 2009); reduced-dose training preserves strength and muscle over short
periods (Spiering 2021), so a brief deload sheds fatigue at little cost.

**Takeaway:** Program a lighter week roughly every 4–8 weeks, or when progress stalls and
fatigue markers stack up (poor sleep, elevated resting HR, dropping performance). A deload
usually means ~half the volume and slightly lighter loads, not full rest. The coach can
recommend one when the app's readiness trend and stalling e1RMs both point to fatigue rather
than under-stimulus.
