**Claim:** To maximize muscle gain from resistance training, total daily protein intake of
roughly 1.6–2.2 g per kg of bodyweight is the key target. More than that adds little for
muscle; less leaves gains on the table.

**Evidence:** A meta-regression found the benefit of added protein plateaued around
~1.6 g/kg/day (Morton 2018); the ISSN position stand recommends ~1.4–2.0 g/kg for building
and maintaining muscle (Jäger 2017).

**Takeaway:** Hitting the daily protein target is the single most important nutrition lever
for muscle. For a user at, say, 75 kg, that's ~120–165 g/day. When the coach diagnoses stalled
growth, verifying protein intake is in range should come before tweaking training. Total
daily intake matters far more than timing or source.
