**Claim:** When losing fat while training, a moderate rate of ~0.5–1% of bodyweight per week
best preserves (or even builds) lean mass and strength. Faster loss increases muscle and
performance loss.

**Evidence:** A slower ~0.7%/week loss increased lean mass and 1RM strength, while a faster
~1.4%/week loss did not (Garthe 2011); Helms 2014 recommends ~0.5–1%/week for physique
athletes.

**Takeaway:** For a user cutting (e.g. toward a 72 kg goal), target a ~0.5–1%/week drop —
for an 80 kg person that's ~0.4–0.8 kg/week — via a modest deficit, high protein, and
continued heavy training. The coach should flag crash diets: losing faster sacrifices the
muscle and strength the training is meant to protect.
