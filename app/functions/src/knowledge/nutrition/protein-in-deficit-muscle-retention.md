**Claim:** During a calorie deficit, raising protein (toward the higher end, ~2.0–2.4 g/kg
bodyweight, or higher relative to lean mass) plus continued resistance training is what
protects muscle — and can even add some — while fat is lost.

**Evidence:** A high-protein group (2.4 g/kg) gained lean mass and lost more fat than a
lower-protein group (1.2 g/kg) in a steep deficit with training (Longland 2016); Helms 2014
recommends elevating protein when dieting (up to ~2.3–3.1 g/kg of lean mass).

**Takeaway:** Cutting is the time to push protein *up*, not down, and to keep lifting heavy
so the body has a reason to keep its muscle. The coach should pair any fat-loss advice with a
higher protein target and maintained training intensity, framing them as the muscle-retention
insurance that makes a cut succeed.
