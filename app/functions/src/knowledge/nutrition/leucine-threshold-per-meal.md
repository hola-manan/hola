**Claim:** A meal needs a minimum "dose" of high-quality protein — roughly 0.4 g/kg
bodyweight, supplying enough leucine — to maximally switch on muscle protein synthesis. Very
small protein snacks under-stimulate MPS.

**Evidence:** MPS follows a dose-response to per-meal protein that saturates around
~0.4 g/kg for most people; the ISSN position stand notes leucine content and per-serving dose
drive the anabolic response (Jäger 2017; Areta 2013).

**Takeaway:** Build each of the day's protein feedings around a proper dose (for a 75 kg
person, ~30 g of quality protein) rather than grazing on tiny amounts. Prioritize
leucine-rich sources (dairy, eggs, meat, whey; or combined plant sources). The coach can use
this to explain why "I eat protein all day" can still miss the mark if no single meal clears
the threshold.
