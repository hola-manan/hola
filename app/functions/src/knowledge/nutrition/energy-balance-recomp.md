**Claim:** Whether bodyweight rises or falls is governed by energy balance (calories in vs
out). Building muscle is easiest in a small surplus and losing fat requires a deficit, but
body *recomposition* (gaining muscle while losing fat) is possible — especially for
beginners, returnees, and higher-body-fat individuals — with high protein and hard training.

**Evidence:** In an aggressive deficit with high protein and intense training, participants
still gained lean mass while losing fat (Longland 2016); Helms 2014 frames calorie level as
the primary determinant of weight change.

**Takeaway:** Set calorie level to the primary goal — modest surplus to prioritize size,
deficit to prioritize leanness — while keeping protein high to protect or build muscle either
way. The coach should tie a user's stated goal (e.g. "cut to 72 kg") to an appropriate
energy-balance direction, not just training changes.
