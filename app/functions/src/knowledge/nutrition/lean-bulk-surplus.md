**Claim:** To build muscle with minimal fat gain, a small energy surplus (~10–20% over
maintenance, ~200–500 kcal) is enough. Larger surpluses accelerate fat gain without
proportionally more muscle, since muscle growth is rate-limited.

**Evidence:** Off-season nutrition recommendations advise a modest surplus targeting
~0.25–0.5% bodyweight gain per week for novice/intermediate lifters, with advanced lifters
gaining even more slowly (Iraki 2019).

**Takeaway:** "Dirty bulking" mostly adds fat you'll later have to cut. Aim for a slow gain
(~0.25–0.5%/week), keep protein at target, and let training drive the muscle. The coach
should temper expectations: muscle is gained slowly, so a bigger surplus buys fat, not speed.
