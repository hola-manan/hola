**Claim:** The post-workout "anabolic window" is hours wide, not minutes. Getting a protein
meal within a few hours around training is sufficient; there is no need to rush a shake
immediately after the last set.

**Evidence:** The ISSN position stand concludes total daily protein and overall distribution
matter more than precise peri-workout timing (Jäger 2017); MPS stays elevated for many hours,
and spacing feedings across recovery works well (Areta 2013).

**Takeaway:** Prioritize hitting the daily protein target and reasonable meal spacing over
stopwatch timing. If someone trains fasted, having a protein meal within a couple of hours
before or after is plenty. The coach should redirect anxiety about immediate post-workout
timing toward the things that actually move the needle: total protein and consistency.
