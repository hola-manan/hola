**Claim:** After a hard session a muscle needs roughly 24–72 hours to recover and complete
its adaptive response before being trained hard again. This recovery window is what sets a
practical ceiling on useful frequency.

**Evidence:** Elevated muscle protein synthesis persists for ~1–2 days post-training (Damas
2016), and ~2×/week frequency (i.e. ~48–72 h spacing) supports growth well (Schoenfeld
2016).

**Takeaway:** Don't train the same muscle hard on back-to-back days; leave ~48 h between
sessions for a given muscle. This is why sensible splits alternate muscle emphasis across
days. When scheduling the cycle, the coach should avoid stacking, say, two heavy pressing
days in a row and instead interleave push/pull/legs so each muscle gets its recovery window.
