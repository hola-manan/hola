**Claim:** A resting heart rate meaningfully above one's 7-day baseline (roughly ≥5 bpm) can
indicate accumulated fatigue, poor sleep, illness, or life stress — a reason to ease off
that day.

**Evidence:** Resting HR and HRV are complementary autonomic markers; deviations from
individual baseline are used in the monitoring literature to flag under-recovery
(Manresa-Rocamora 2021 and the broader autonomic-monitoring evidence).

**Takeaway:** Judge resting HR against the person's own rolling baseline, not a population
number. The app already encodes this: `rhrElevated` flags a resting HR ≥5 bpm over the
7-day average, and that flag feeds the low-readiness assessment. The coach should read an
elevated-RHR day as a cue to reduce intensity or check for illness — especially if sleep is
also poor.
