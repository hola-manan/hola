**Claim:** Recovery fluctuates day to day, so a fixed plan will sometimes ask for more than
the body can give. Trimming intensity/volume on a clearly under-recovered day preserves the
training stimulus while avoiding digging a deeper fatigue hole.

**Evidence:** Sleep loss lowers performance capacity for that day (Craven 2022), and
readiness-guided approaches steer hard work toward recovered days for better outcomes
(Manresa-Rocamora 2021).

**Takeaway:** On a low-readiness day (poor sleep, low energy, or elevated resting HR),
reduce load ~10–15% or shift the hard session — don't force it. The app operationalizes this
with a single low-readiness rule (`isLowReadiness`) that trims intensity ~12%; the coach's
day-to-day advice should agree with that assessment rather than override it.
