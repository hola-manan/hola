**Claim:** Extending sleep toward ~8–10 hours improves athletic performance — speed,
accuracy, reaction time, and mood — in people who were previously under-slept. Sleep is a
performance enhancer, not just damage control.

**Evidence:** When collegiate basketball players extended sleep for several weeks, sprint
times, shooting accuracy, reaction time, and mood all improved significantly (Mah 2011).

**Takeaway:** For someone chasing progress, prioritizing sleep is one of the
highest-leverage, lowest-cost interventions available — often more impactful than tweaking
the program. The coach should treat consistently short sleep (visible in the app's readiness
history) as a first-order thing to address before adding training volume.
