**Claim:** Psychological and lifestyle stress (work pressure, poor sleep, under-eating) draw
on the same recovery capacity as training. High life stress slows recovery and adaptation,
so training tolerance drops during stressful periods.

**Evidence:** Sleep loss — a common consequence and correlate of stress — directly impairs
performance and increases perceived effort (Craven 2022); stress and recovery share
physiological pathways in the broader monitoring literature.

**Takeaway:** When life is chaotic, expect lower training capacity and program more
conservatively — hold volume steady or reduce it rather than pushing PRs. The app's manual
energy check-in and note field capture this subjective load; the coach should factor a run of
low-energy/high-stress days into recommendations, not just the objective watch metrics.
