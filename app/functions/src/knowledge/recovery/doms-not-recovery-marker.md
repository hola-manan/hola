**Claim:** Delayed-onset muscle soreness (DOMS) is a weak, unreliable indicator of recovery
or of whether a muscle is ready to train. You can be sore yet recovered, or recovered yet
still growing without soreness.

**Evidence:** Soreness tracks unaccustomed stimulus and muscle damage, which is dissociated
from the hypertrophic adaptation itself (Damas 2016); it fades as a muscle adapts to a
given exercise regardless of ongoing progress.

**Takeaway:** Don't use soreness to decide whether to train. Better readiness signals are
performance (can you hit your numbers?), sleep, energy, and resting-HR trend — the inputs the
app's readiness record actually uses. The coach should redirect users who gauge recovery by
soreness toward these more meaningful markers.
