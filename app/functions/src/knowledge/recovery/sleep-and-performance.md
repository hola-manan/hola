**Claim:** Short or poor sleep measurably impairs physical performance — including strength,
power, and high-intensity work — and raises perceived effort, so a hard session on little
sleep is both harder and less productive.

**Evidence:** A meta-analytical review found acute sleep loss reduces physical performance
across multiple domains, with next-day and afternoon sessions particularly affected (Craven
2022).

**Takeaway:** Treat a bad night as a real signal, not an excuse to ignore. On low-sleep
days, expect lower output and either reduce intensity/volume or swap to easier work — which
is exactly what the app's low-readiness rule does (it trims intensity ~12% when sleep is
poor). The coach should connect a poor-sleep readiness record to a lighter recommendation
for that day.
