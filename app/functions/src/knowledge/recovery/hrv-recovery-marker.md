**Claim:** Overnight heart-rate variability (HRV) reflects autonomic recovery state and can
help guide training load. Higher-than-usual HRV suggests readiness; a suppressed reading
suggests accumulated stress or under-recovery — but only relative to the individual's own
baseline and trend.

**Evidence:** HRV-guided training can match or modestly outperform fixed plans by steering
hard sessions toward recovered days, though effects are moderate and methodology-dependent
(Manresa-Rocamora 2021).

**Takeaway:** Use HRV as a *trend* against personal baseline, never as a single-day verdict —
it's noisy and affected by alcohol, illness, and measurement conditions. This is why the
app's readiness record combines the watch's HRV/readiness score with sleep and a manual
energy check-in rather than relying on HRV alone. The coach should weigh the whole readiness
picture.
